Mental Health Information

Everything you need to know about mental health services, conditions, and how Better Space can support you on your journey.

What is Mental Health?

Mental health refers to your overall emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how you think, feel, and act — and plays a role in how you handle stress, relate to others, and make choices every day.

Good mental health does not mean the absence of problems. It means having the tools and support to cope with life's challenges in a healthy way.

Mental health matters just as much as physical health. Seeking support is a sign of strength, not weakness.

Common Mental Health Conditions

Anxiety Depression Stress PTSD OCD Eating Disorders Sleep Issues Low Self-Esteem

Anxiety

Anxiety is an excessive worry or fear about everyday situations. It can show up as racing thoughts, muscle tension, or difficulty sleeping. Treatment often involves therapy, coping techniques, and sometimes medication.

Depression

Depression is more than sadness — it is a persistent feeling of emptiness or hopelessness that affects your ability to function. A professional can help you work through it with the right support plan.

Stress

Everyone experiences stress, but when it becomes overwhelming or long-lasting it can affect your health. Learning to manage stress through regular routines, exercise, and talking to someone is key.

If you recognise signs of any of these conditions in yourself, our mental health professionals on Better Space are here to help. Use the search page to find someone in your area of need.

Types of Therapy Available

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT)

CBT helps you identify and change negative thought patterns. It is one of the most evidence-based approaches for anxiety, depression, and stress.

Talk Therapy / Counselling

A safe space to express your feelings and work through challenges with a trained professional. Great for building self-awareness and emotional resilience.

Mindfulness-Based Therapy

Focuses on staying present and aware. Techniques like guided breathing and meditation can reduce anxiety and improve overall well-being.

Trauma-Focused Therapy

Specifically designed to help individuals process and recover from traumatic experiences at their own pace.

Not sure which type of therapy is right for you? Our professionals will work with you during your first session to find the best fit.

Coping Strategies & Self-Care

Breathe Move Sleep Connect Journal Limit screens

Deep Breathing

Slow, deep breaths activate your body's relaxation response. Try breathing in for 4 counts, holding for 4, and out for 6. Repeat several times when you feel overwhelmed.

Regular Exercise

Even a short walk can reduce stress hormones and boost your mood. Aim for at least 20–30 minutes of movement each day.

Healthy Sleep

Sleep is when your mind recovers. Keep a consistent schedule, limit screens before bed, and create a calm environment.

Talk to Someone

Sharing how you feel — with a friend, family member, or professional — can lighten the weight you carry. You do not have to do it alone.

Journalling

Writing down your thoughts helps you process emotions and spot patterns in your mood over time.

Reach out immediately if you are experiencing thoughts of self-harm or suicide, feeling unable to keep yourself safe, or going through a mental health emergency.
